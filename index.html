26 September 2022
exploring.

of course i have preconceived notions of what i’ll find and what i’ll end up making for this whole thesis process.

and of course within two days what i thought i would be doing has been turned around.

one week later, turned around again.

another week later, changed again.

not ideal for someone with anxiety, but tis the nature of research. and i'm really grateful to learn.

this general topic of ableism and empathy spoke to me because of the heightened marginalisation i’ve seen throughout this ongoing pandemic.

when those with disabilities spoke out about unsafe spaces when health protections dropped and they were told to just stay home permanently. told that their lives aren’t important enough. i wanted to do something.

marginalisation and lack of access have always been an issue and progress has been made, but the west has taken a huge step back. economy over lives, of course.

the increased accessibility we finally figured out how to implement with the solutions we used in the early days abandoned and leaving inclusivity behind with it.

i really didn’t think i would find that children show the most ableism. i thought it would be middle aged people, but middle aged people have been shown to have the lowest levels. huh.

i thought i would be doing a general education campaign, but that isn’t enough so I need to concentrate on the population that needs the most help to combat discrimination and ableism.

so what i saw in my head as an interactive campaign for adults has been adjusted to a digital and print curriculum piece for kids.

back to the research. hoping that this will be the final plan, but, as always, wanting to know more and wishing i could download all of the available information into my brain! that would be more efficient time-wise 😊
